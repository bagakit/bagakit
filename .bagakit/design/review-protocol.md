# Design Review Protocol

`bagakit-design-core` uses one design packet across three checkpoints.

## Draft Review

Before implementation or final image prompting, check:

- target register is explicit
- source evidence and confidence are recorded
- tone axes are concrete enough to build from
- reference tier and cannot-lose qualities are named
- design rules cover hierarchy, density, copy, states, accessibility, and
  generic-output risks

## Concrete Plan Review

Before build work, check:

- tokens, geometry, components, states, assets, and responsive behavior are
  specific enough to avoid visual guessing
- observed, derived, and fallback choices remain distinct
- proprietary assets are treated as references or user-provided assets, not
  redistributed substitutes
- the implementation skill knows which packet fields it must preserve

## Result Review

Before completion, check:

- the final result preserves tone, register fit, and reference tier
- design-rule blockers are resolved or explicitly downgraded
- screenshots or generated outputs do not regress into generic, over-padded,
  verbose, low-density, or decorative design
- accepted uncertainty is still acceptable after seeing the result
