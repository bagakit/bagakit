# Media Production Family

This family owns end-to-end workflows that turn inputs into audience-facing
media products through research, planning, organization, production, quality
gates, publishing, distribution, notification, and archival.

Media-production skills compose adjacent capabilities without absorbing them:

- research evidence remains owned by researcher skills
- writing and rewrite quality remains owned by paperwork skills
- visual and webpage craft remains owned by design skills
- provider-specific deployment and notification remains owned by their tools
  or adapters
- learning and reflection outcomes remain owned by human-improvement skills

Use this family for a real media-production co-evolution boundary, not for any
workflow that happens to be automated. Individual skills should keep specific
identities and trigger boundaries even when they share production-stage,
receipt, quality-gate, and publication semantics.
